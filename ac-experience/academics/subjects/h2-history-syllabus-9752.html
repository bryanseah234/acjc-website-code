H2 History (Syllabus 9752)

History at ACJC creates strong foundations in historical thinking skills and hopes to foster enthusiasm about the past. The teaching programme is designed to create young historians, who can critically consider the major developments in twentieth century world affairs.

Consonant with the desired national outcomes of education, the young historian, upon completion of the History course at ACJC must also have the moral temerity to stand up for what is right, empathize with people living in diverse places at different times, and understand Singapore in relation to the world. Our programme provides our students with sufficient historical understanding for them to appreciate the problems and possibilities in our troubled world long after they have graduated.

The core mode of instruction is the lecture and tutorial system. Lectures are meant to inspire thoughtful speculation into historical narratives and to trigger introspection into deeper moral and ethical concerns confronting humanity at large. The tutorial sessions open the causes and consequences as well as the assessment of broader trends in history to debate. At times, this can take the form of seminars where collaborative and independent learning takes centre stage. Extra-curricular consultations, with individualized instruction and mentoring, can be made available at the request of the students.


 H2 History provides opportunities to develop students’ historical knowledge, core skills and understanding of the subject’s disciplinarity. Emphasis is given to developing conceptual understanding of historical knowledge. This approach encourages students to gain depth of understanding in historical events and their connections to the present. This will also be achieved through the emphasis on historical agency, allowing students to appreciate the complexities of multiple perspectives and the dynamic interactions between diverse actors in understanding local, regional and global developments.

 

H2 History consists of 2 papers:

Paper 1 - Shaping the International Order (1945–2000)

This paper examines the attempts of the superpowers and various other historical actors to shape the international order after the Second World War, which resulted in key global developments in the second half of the twentieth century. These developments highlight the historical agency of local and regional actors in the evolution of the global social, political and economic order.  These key developments and events will be studied in the context of the following themes:

·         Understanding the Cold War, 1945–1991 (source-based case study)

·         Understanding the Global Economy, 1945–2000

·         Safeguarding International Peace and Security

Paper 2 - The Making of Independent Southeast Asia (Independence–2000)

The thematic-comparative approach in this paper will expose students to a richer and more multifaceted view of independent Southeast Asia. Studying how newly independent states and their people sought to assert their place and identity amidst the forces of change will provide the historical context to understand present-day Southeast Asia. Through the themes, students will evaluate the successes and limitations of Southeast Asian countries’ search for domestic stability as well as the pursuit of economic development after independence. Students will also be able to gain a better understanding of the patterns of interactions within the region and between different groups of actors within the state. The contestation between authoritarianism and the agency of the people will also be studied to understand the dynamic unfolding of events in independent Southeast Asia.

The themes will be studied in the context of the following:

·         Search for Political Stability

·         Economic Development after Independence

·         Regional Conflicts and Cooperation (source-based case study)

 

The assessment for H2 History consists of two 3 hour examination with 2 sections for each paper:

·         Section A: 2 compulsory source-based question

·         Section B: 2 Essay Questions
