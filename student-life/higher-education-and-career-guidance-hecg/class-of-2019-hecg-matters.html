B. Etiquette for requesting reference letters


  1. Which applications require teachers’ references? 

  • Local universities will require online submission of teachers’ reference for the following applications 

  • NUS Medicine *

  • NTU Medicine *

  • NTU Renaissance Engineering Programme (REP) *

  • SUTD courses * or ^

  • Scholarships *

  • Discretionary admission to local AUs ^

  • For applications that ask for a testimonial, please submit a copy or scan of your School Graduation Certificate (SGC).

  • Who to ask for your reference letter: 

* Subject teachers, especially those who taught you in a related subject

^ CCA / VIA teachers who have a good understanding of your talents


  1. Guidelines for requesting teacher references:

  • Give early notice – A good reference takes time to prepare!

  • Provide information – Give full details about the type of reference (academic or holistic), deadline of submission, mode of submission (email, online portal or post) 

  • Communicate – Share your motivations in person or through sharing your personal statement with your teacher so that the reference letter is in agreement with your application, wherever possible. 

  • Update – It is good courtesy to update your teacher referee(s) about the outcome of your applications.

II. Common App


For students that applied via CommonApp in 2019

You may log in to check your application @ https://www.commonapp.org/

Successful applicants are typically notified between March and April.

If you are a successful applicant who has received more than one (1) offer to a university;

(1)    Take your time to look at each university more closely

(2)    Factor in the location, accommodation and cost in making your decision

(3)    Make your decision

(4)    INFORM YOUR TEACHER WHICH UNIVERSITY OPTION YOU WILL BE TAKING UP

(5)    SUBMIT YOUR DIGITAL CERTIFICATE IN OpenCerts FORMAT TO YOUR TEACHER. This is available from MySkillFuture’s Skills Passport from 24 Feb 2020. 

(6)    YOUR TEACHER WILL SEND THE CERTIFICATE TO THE UNIVERSITY THAT YOU HAVE DECIDED IN POINT (3)

(7)    Your teacher will then proceed to send your results to that university of your choice

(8)    Start looking to applying for your student Visa (F1 student visa)

If you are unsuccessful in getting the university of your choice;

(1)    See if you have received other offers from the universities

(2)    You may begin your application for local universities or

(3)    You may apply for the CommonApp 2020

 

For students intending to apply via CommonApp in 2020

Dates to take note of if you are applying for the CommonApp in 2020.

CommonApp 2020 goes live – 1 August 2020

CommonApp2020 early action deadline – 1 November 2020

CommonApp2020 Regular decision deadline – 1 January 2021
