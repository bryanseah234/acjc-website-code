Highlights of the Class of 2015

The College is proud to feature the following students who exemplify the College’s Vision of Noble Ambition and Character, for the Service of God and Nation.

Daniel Tan Yuan An (Alumnus, Anglo-Chinese School Barker Road)

A member of the ACJC Choir, Daniel was proud to have represented the College as part of their Distinction winning performance at the Singapore Youth Festival Central Judging. He was further privileged to have represented the College at the ACJC-Tezukayama High School Cultural Exchange to Nra and Osaka.

Daniel was the Values-In-Action (VIA) representative for his class, in which role he coordinated and acted as project leader for two class initiated VIA projects: the school’s annual Health Week in 2014 and Project Cheer in 2015. For Health Week, he and his class worked with the Bone Marrow Donor Programme to organise a week of booths and showcases to spread awareness about bone marrow donation in Singapore and encourage the school population to become bone marrow donors and aid the cause.

Daniel pursued his passion for the sciences by familiarising himself with their applicability to the workplace through two work attachments, one at A*STAR’s Institute of Bioengineering and Nanotechnology as part of the A*STAR Scholarship and the other at Orthopaedic ‘A’ Care services, an orthopaedic clinic in Gleneagles as part of the school’s job experience programme. His internship at A*STAR lasted 5 weeks where he worked with a scientific researcher. His medical internship at the clinic lasted 6 days where he was able to discover the workings of a private clinic and shadow the daily activities of an orthopaedic surgeon in private practice. Daniel scored a total of 8 distinctions at the 2015 GCE A-level examinations, in H2 Biology, Chemistry, Economics and Mathematics, H1 General Paper, Mother Tongue and Project Work, and H3 Molecular Biology.

Le Thi An Lanh (Alumna, CHIJ Toa Payoh)

A science student in secondary school with no background in History, she made the bold move to specialise in the Humanities at junior college. Intellectually motivated and independent, An Lanh brought new ideas and questions to class and engaged in such discussions beyond the classroom.

For her academic excellence, An Lanh received the Wan Fook Weng Award for being the top student in the JC1 Promotional Examination, together with book prizes in H2 History, H2 Economics and H1 Literature in English. In addition, she has been consistently in her academic achievements and been placed on the list of top students for numerous major examinations. She continued this outstanding performance at the 2015 GCE A-levels, scoring 7 distinctions, in H2 Economics, History, Knowledge and Inquiry, Mathematics, H1 Literature in English, Project Work, and H3 History.

Her passion for the English Language is evident in the tenacity displayed as she sought to improve her command of it and was an important member of the Debating and Oratorical Society. Her efforts earned her the respect of both peers and teachers alike and this led to her appointment as the team’s acting Vice-Captain in 2015. Passionate about debate, An Lanh was determined to pay forward the support she had received by investing her time in her junior debaters to help them improve in their debating skills. She received the Outstanding Service Award on Honours Night for her contributions to Debate.

Yeo Kang Sheng (Alumnus, Anglo-Chinese School Barker Road)

Kang Sheng has capped his commendable academic record at ACJC by scoring 4 distinctions in H2 Geography, H2 Mathematics, H1 Economics, and H1 General Paper. Born with cerebral palsy, Kang Sheng has met the challenges he has faced with cheerful optimism. He has lived the ACJC core value of tenacity, proving that where there’s a will, there’s a way.

Kang Sheng is a young man of integrity, well loved and respected by his peers, and dedicated to serving others. He engaged in values in action projects such as FaithActs and Project Cheer. He also helped his classmates with their academic work. For his ever inspiring resilience in the face of adversity, Kang Sheng received the ACJC Courage Award in 2015.


Sheryl Lim Jing Yi (Alumna, Nan Chiau High School)

Sheryl was the President of the ACJC Dance Society. Her steadfast and inspirational leadership was clearly evident as she led the Dance Society to a Certificate of Distinction at the Singapore Youth Festival Arts Presentation 2015. In addition, she headed the production team for the annual dance concert, Bailamos 2015. Sheryl was also highly involved in college-based performances such as Open House 2015, the Co-Curricular Activities Bazaar and International Friendship Day Concert. Out of college, she performed at the NUSS Lunar New Year Celebrations at the Kent Ridge Guild House.

Beyond her college-based commitments, Sheryl also took ballet lessons at the Nanyang Academy of Fine Arts (NAFA) Junior Department, and was awarded Merit for the Royal Dance Academy (RAD) Intermediate Vocational Level Examination. She also played a lead role in the NAFA Biennial Concert and NAFA 50th Anniversary Celebrations in 2014.

Despite her busy schedule, Sheryl is a regular volunteer with the Metropolitan YMCA as a children’s camp facilitator. Her self-discipline ensured that her commitment to her schoolwork never took a backseat. Sheryl Lim was a recipient of the Class of 2002 Alumni Scholarship and was on the Top Students list for all major examinations. She was placed on the prestigious Principal’s Honour Roll in recognition of her all-round achievements. At the 2015 GCE A-level examinations, she attained distinctions in H2 Chemistry, Geography, Physics, and H1 Mathematics.

Karthigan S/O Ramatas (Alumnus, Gan Eng Seng School)

Karthigan is an industrious and dedicated student. Crowning his academic achievements throughout his career at ACJC, Karthigan has achieved a total of 6 distinctions at the GCE ‘A’-levels, 2015, in H2 Tamil Language & Literature, H2 Economics, H2 Biology, H1 Mathematics, H1 General Paper and H1 Project Work. Excelling in the Tamil Language, Karthigan was a recipient of the prestigious National Elective Tamil Language Programme Scholarship. His passion for language and the arts is evident through the awards he has won for poetry, acting, and Indian dance.

Karthigan has overcome adversity in his JC life, having to be hospitalized for a month after being diagnosed with a liver abscess in 2015. This disruption notwithstanding, Karthigan worked hard to catch up on what he had missed. His tenacity won him the ACJC Courage award in 2015.

Along with passion and tenacity, Karthigan demonstrated integrity as a leader, serving as President of the Tamil Language Society, and working hard to share his own knowledge and acumen, facilitating the 2015 National Tamil Story-Telling Competition for primary school students as well as working with SINDA to help organise the 2015 National Futsal Challenge.

Thng Ming Li Millie (Alumna, School of Science and Technology)

Millie was a recipient of the A*STAR Science Award (Junior College) from 2014 to 2015. For her outstanding academic achievements, she received the Edusave Certificate of Academic Achievement awarded by the Ministry of Education in 2014. At the 2015 GCE A-level examinations, Millie attained distinctions in H2 Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics and H1 Project Work. She participated in the 2014/2015 Research Mentorship Programme organised by Nanyang Technology University’s School of Materials Science and Engineering (NTU MSE), where her team achieved a Merit Award. She presented her team’s research work at NTU MSE’s Materials Awareness Day 2015 and ACJC’s Science and Innovation Symposium 2015. Her interest in Science and Technology also saw her participation in the ACJC Science Essay Competition 2015, where she was awarded the Merit Prize.

Millie also volunteered her services for the College’s Science and Mathematics Council. She showed good interpersonal and communication skills as she facilitated discussions on experiments carried out by primary school students during the 2015 “Little Professor” research event for primary school students.

For her outstanding services in leading the Guitar Ensemble, Millie was awarded the Anglo-Chinese Junior College's Honours Night Outstanding Service Award. Millie demonstrated good leadership through her organisation and execution of major events, such as the annual concert “Glissando” 2015. The Guitar Ensemble also clinched the Certificate of Accomplishment in the Singapore Youth Festival 2015.

Millie also participated actively in her class VIA activities, specifically ‘Project Cheer’ with an external organization, Tan Chin Tuan Foundation, in 2014 and 2015. She helped to plan and facilitate the activities for interaction between children from Lakeside Family Centre and elderly from St Luke’s Eldercare (Jurong East Centre).

Outside of college, Millie also volunteered regularly at TOUCH Young Arrows (TYA), a service under TOUCH Community Services, giving weekly tuition on English and Mathematics to primary school children from low income families and organising events such as TYA Games Day 2014 and 2015, and TYA Family Tour 2014.

Andrew Ng Jun De (Alumnus, Fairfield Methodist Secondary School)

Andrew is a sincere leader and an erudite scholar who serves the school community faithfully. Andrew was a recipient of the Class of 2002 Alumni Scholarship. He was among the top students for the 2014 Promotional Examinations and also received the book prize for General Paper. At the 2015 GCE A-level examinations, Andrew attained distinctions in H2 Art, Economics, History, H1 General Paper, Mathematics, Project Work, and H3 History.

Andrew was the Captain of the ACJC Debate & Oratorical Society and a member of the champion team for the Nanyang Technological University Dorothy Chung Debate Championships 2014. Andrew was also ranked amongst the Top 10 speakers for the Singapore Red Cross International Humanitarian Law Debates 2014. In 2015, he led the team which represented the college at the international Bratislava Schools Debating Championships in Slovakia. The team emerged as international 1st runners-up. As the Captain, Andrew played a key role in organising and executing workshops and competitions for the Singaporean debating circuit. These include public speaking workshops for primary school children at Mayflower Primary School, the LEXIS Masterclass Series debating workshop targeted at secondary and primary school students and competitions such as the inaugural International Oldham Cup Debate Competition and the MOE-ACJC Debating Championships 2015. A trained adjudicator, Andrew has contributed to the local debating circuit by assisting as an adjudicator for the Singapore Secondary Schools Debate Competition 2015.

Andrew also has a love of Science and, through the ACJC Science and Mathematics Council, volunteered as an emcee for the Singapore Engineering Physics Challenge, a national physics competition for the secondary school level. He was also a chief invigilator for the International C.B Paul Science Quiz.
